## Emergency Categories

### IMMEDIATE ACTION (call contractor now, notify owner after)

These are safety/habitability emergencies. Act first, get approval later.

- **Water emergency:** Burst pipe, major leak, flooding
  - Step 1: Tell tenant to turn off water main (document location per property)
  - Step 2: Call emergency plumber
  - Step 3: Notify owner
  - Step 4: Document with photos

- **No heat (winter, below 50F):** Habitability requirement in most states
  - Step 1: Call HVAC emergency service
  - Step 2: If resolution will take >24 hours, provide space heater to tenant
  - Step 3: Notify owner

- **Gas smell:**
  - Step 1: Tell tenant to evacuate immediately, do NOT use light switches
  - Step 2: Call gas company emergency line
  - Step 3: Call 911 if strong smell
  - Step 4: Notify owner

- **Electrical hazard:** Sparking, burning smell, exposed wiring
  - Step 1: Tell tenant to turn off breaker for affected circuit
  - Step 2: Call emergency electrician
  - Step 3: Notify owner

- **Fire:**
  - Step 1: Tenant calls 911 and evacuates
  - Step 2: Notify owner immediately
  - Step 3: Contact insurance company within 24 hours
  - Step 4: Document everything with photos once safe

- **Break-in / security breach:**
  - Step 1: Tenant calls 911
  - Step 2: Notify owner
  - Step 3: Call locksmith to re-secure the property
  - Step 4: File police report

### URGENT (respond within 24 hours)

- Toilet not flushing (only toilet in unit)
- Refrigerator not working (food safety)
- Exterior door won't lock
- Significant roof leak (during rain)
- Pest infestation (health risk -- e.g., rodents, bedbugs)

### ROUTINE (schedule within 3-7 days)

- Minor plumbing (dripping faucet, slow drain)
- Appliance issue (dishwasher, garbage disposal)
- Cosmetic damage
- Non-urgent maintenance

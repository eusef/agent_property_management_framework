## Step-by-Step: Non-Payment Eviction

### Step 1: Verify the Facts
- [ ] Confirm rent is actually past due (check income records)
- [ ] Confirm amount owed (include any applicable late fees per lease)
- [ ] Confirm no payment was received and misapplied
- [ ] Note the exact number of days past due

### Step 2: Serve [YOUR_STATE] Non-Payment Notice
- [ ] Use [YOUR_STATE] official form or attorney-drafted notice
- [ ] Notice must state: amount owed, deadline to pay, consequence of non-payment
- [ ] Include the exact amount owed (rent + any fees outlined in lease)
- [ ] Serve properly (see Service Methods below)
- [ ] Document the date, time, and method of service
- [ ] Keep a copy of the notice

### Step 3: Wait the Full Notice Period
- [ ] Count begins the day after service (not the day of service) -- verify for [YOUR_STATE]
- [ ] If tenant pays in full within the notice period, the notice is void -- accept payment
- [ ] If tenant offers partial payment: consult a lawyer about whether accepting waives the notice
- [ ] Do NOT accept partial payment without legal advice

### Step 4: If Not Cured -- File Eviction Complaint
- [ ] Notice period has expired with no payment
- [ ] File eviction complaint in [YOUR_STATE_COURT]
- [ ] Pay filing fee
- [ ] Court hearing typically scheduled within [YOUR_STATE_TIMEFRAME]
- [ ] Strongly recommend hiring an attorney at this point

### Step 5: Court Hearing
- [ ] Bring: lease, notice (with proof of service), payment records, any communication records
- [ ] If judgment in your favor: court issues a judgment of restitution
- [ ] Tenant has [YOUR_STATE_PERIOD] days to vacate after judgment
- [ ] If tenant does not vacate: sheriff/constable executes the eviction (landlord may NOT self-help)

## Step-by-Step: Lease Violation Eviction

### Step 1: Document the Violation
- [ ] Identify the specific lease clause being violated
- [ ] Gather evidence (photos, written complaints, records)
- [ ] Determine if this is a first violation or a repeat

### Step 2: Serve Violation Notice (First Violation)
- [ ] Notice must state: the specific violation, what tenant must do to cure, cure deadline, and that tenancy terminates if not cured
- [ ] Serve properly (see Service Methods below)
- [ ] Document service

### Step 3: Monitor Cure Period
- [ ] If tenant cures within the cure period: notice is void, document the cure
- [ ] If same violation recurs within [YOUR_STATE_PERIOD]: may serve a no-cure notice

### Step 4: If Not Cured -- File Eviction
- [ ] Same process as non-payment (Step 4-5 above)

## What You Cannot Do

- **No self-help eviction.** You cannot change locks, shut off utilities, remove doors/windows, or remove tenant's belongings. This is illegal in [YOUR_STATE] and can result in penalties.
- **No retaliation.** You cannot evict a tenant for requesting repairs, complaining to a government agency, or joining a tenant organization. [YOUR_STATE_STATUTE]
- **No discrimination.** Eviction criteria must be applied consistently. See compliance/fair-housing.md.

## When to Call a Lawyer

- Any contested eviction (tenant disputes the notice or responds to complaint)
- If tenant claims retaliation or discrimination
- If tenant is in a protected class and claims the eviction is pretextual
- If tenant has a Section 8 voucher (additional procedural requirements)
- Any time you're unsure about the process
- Domestic violence situations (tenant may have additional rights)

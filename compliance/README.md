# Compliance

**Purpose:** This folder contains compliance templates and reference documents for property management legal obligations. All documents are jurisdiction-agnostic templates that you must customize for your state, county, and city.

---

## How to Use This Section

1. **Read each file** and fill in the `[YOUR_STATE]` placeholders with your jurisdiction's specific laws, statutes, and requirements.
2. **Consult a lawyer** before relying on any of these documents for legal actions. These are operational references, not legal advice.
3. **Check for local additions** -- many cities and counties layer additional requirements on top of state law (rent control, rental registration, relocation assistance, etc.). Document those in your jurisdiction-specific files.
4. **Review annually** -- landlord-tenant law changes frequently. Set a calendar reminder to review all compliance docs at least once a year.
5. **See `examples/oregon/`** for a worked example of how one state's laws map to these templates.

## Important Disclaimer

These documents are operational references created to help organize your property management compliance. They are **not legal advice**. Laws vary by state, county, and city, and change frequently. Always consult a qualified attorney licensed in your jurisdiction before:

- Issuing eviction notices
- Making security deposit deductions
- Screening tenants
- Raising rent
- Terminating a tenancy
- Any situation where legal rights are at stake

---

## Getting Started

1. Start with `landlord-tenant-law.md` -- research and fill in your state's key statutes
2. Fill in `security-deposits.md` with your state's deposit rules
3. Complete `eviction.md` with your state's notice periods and procedures
4. Review `fair-housing.md` and add any state-level protected classes
5. Set up `calendar.md` with your specific deadlines
6. Customize `insurance-tracker.md` with your actual policies

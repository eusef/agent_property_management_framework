# Property Standards Guide

**Purpose:** Standardize materials and fixtures across your portfolio to reduce maintenance costs, simplify repairs, and attract quality tenants. The condition of your property directly determines the quality of tenant it attracts.

---

## Core Principle

Invest in durability and standardization. When every property uses the same faucet, the same paint color, and the same flooring, you carry fewer spare parts, your contractors know what to expect, and turnovers go faster.

---

## Recommended Standards by Category

### Plumbing Fixtures

| Item | Recommendation | Rationale |
|------|---------------|-----------|
| Faucets (kitchen and bath) | Standardize on one brand and model across all units (e.g., Moen) | All use the same cartridge. One spare cartridge covers the entire portfolio. Reduces inventory and simplifies repairs. |
| Toilets | Standard two-piece, comfort height | Widely available, easy to find replacement parts. Comfort height is preferred by most tenants. |
| Toilet seats | Replace at every turnover | Cheap ($15-25), high visual impact. Stained seats make a clean bathroom look dirty. |
| Garbage disposals | Consider removing | Disposals are one of the top sources of maintenance calls. No disposal = no disposal problems. Cap the drain opening. |
| Shower heads | Basic chrome, standard thread | Tenants can upgrade if they want. Easy to replace at turnover. |

### Flooring

| Type | Where to Use | Rationale |
|------|-------------|-----------|
| Luxury Vinyl Plank (LVT/LVP) | Living areas, bedrooms, hallways | Waterproof, durable, easy to clean, easy to replace individual planks. Lower long-term cost than carpet. |
| Tile | Bathrooms, kitchens, entryways | Best for wet areas. Choose neutral colors. |
| Carpet | Avoid where possible | Stains, wears, requires professional cleaning between tenants, and often needs full replacement every 3-5 years in rentals. If you must use carpet, go with a neutral color and medium-grade commercial carpet. |

### Paint

| Item | Recommendation |
|------|---------------|
| Color | Neutral throughout (e.g., Agreeable Gray, Swiss Coffee). One color for walls, one for trim, across all properties. |
| Sheen - walls | Eggshell or satin (wipeable, hides imperfections) |
| Sheen - trim/doors | Semi-gloss (durable, easy to clean) |
| Sheen - bathrooms/kitchen | Satin or semi-gloss (moisture resistant) |
| Primer for odors | Shellac-based primer (e.g., Zinsser BIN) before painting over smoke or pet odor stains |

### HVAC

| Item | Recommendation |
|------|---------------|
| System | Standard efficiency is fine for rentals. Higher-efficiency systems cost more upfront with marginal benefit when the tenant pays utilities. |
| Filters | Specify filter size in the property README. Require tenants to change monthly (or provide filters and charge a fee for dirty filters at inspection). |
| Thermostat | Programmable or smart thermostat. Prevents extreme temperature settings that stress the system. |

### Appliances

| Item | Recommendation |
|------|---------------|
| Brand consistency | Pick one mid-range brand and standardize. Reduces the number of repair manuals and parts needed. |
| Dishwasher | Standard built-in. Replace when repair cost exceeds 50% of replacement cost. |
| Range/oven | Standard electric or gas (match existing hookup). |
| Refrigerator | Standard top-freezer or bottom-freezer. No ice makers (maintenance headache). |
| Washer/dryer | If provided: standard top-load washer, basic electric dryer. Consider whether providing these is worth the maintenance burden vs. hookups only. |

### Safety

| Item | Standard |
|------|---------|
| Smoke detectors | Hardwired with battery backup on every floor and in every bedroom. Replace batteries at every turnover and annual inspection. Replace units every 10 years. |
| CO detectors | Required near sleeping areas in most states. Check your state law. |
| Deadbolts | Grade 2 or better on all exterior doors. Re-key between tenants. |
| Exterior lighting | Motion-activated LED. Bright exterior lighting deters criminal activity and reduces liability. |
| Fire extinguisher | Consider providing one in the kitchen. Not required in most states for SFH, but good practice. |

### Exterior

| Item | Recommendation |
|------|---------------|
| Landscaping | Low-maintenance. Drought-tolerant plants where possible. Define clearly in the lease who is responsible for yard maintenance. |
| Gutters | Clean twice per year (spring and fall). Clogged gutters cause foundation and water damage. |
| Roof | Inspect annually. Budget for replacement based on material lifespan (asphalt shingles: 20-30 years). |
| Siding | Pressure wash annually or as needed. |

## Replacement Planning

Use the maintenance forecast in each property's README to plan replacements:

| Item | Typical Lifespan | Estimated Cost |
|------|-----------------|----------------|
| Roof (asphalt shingles) | 20-30 years | $8,000-15,000 |
| HVAC system | 15-20 years | $5,000-10,000 |
| Water heater (tank) | 10-12 years | $1,000-2,000 |
| Exterior paint | 7-10 years | $3,000-6,000 |
| Carpet (if used) | 3-5 years in rentals | $1,500-3,000 |
| Appliances | 10-15 years | $500-1,500 each |
| Toilets | 25+ years | $200-400 |
| Faucets | 15-20 years | $100-300 |

**Budget rule of thumb:** Reserve approximately 1% of property value per year for maintenance and replacements.

# Move-In Welcome Letter

**When to use:** Send to new tenant on or before move-in day. Include with keys and any access information.
**Purpose:** Sets the relationship on a professional, friendly footing. Provides all essential info in one place so the tenant doesn't have to ask.

---

## Welcome Letter

Subject: Welcome to [Property Address]

Hi [Tenant Name],

Welcome to your new home at [Property Address]! I'm Phil Johnston, your landlord, and I'm happy to have you.

Here's everything you need to get settled:

**Contact Information**
- For maintenance requests or questions: [Phone] or [Email]
- For emergencies (water leak, fire, no heat, safety issue): Call [Emergency Phone] immediately

**Important Details**
- Rent: $[Amount] due on the 1st of each month
- Payment method: [Zelle to X / Check mailed to Y / etc.]
- Late fee: $[Amount] after the [Grace Period Day]th
- Lease term: [Start Date] to [End Date]

**Utilities**
- You are responsible for: [Electric, Gas, Internet, etc.]
- I cover: [Water, Sewer, Trash, etc.]
- Utility companies to set up: [Company names and phone numbers]

**Property Information**
- Trash/recycling pickup day: [Day]
- Parking: [Details]
- Laundry: [Details]
- Mail: [Mailbox location/number]
- [Any HOA rules or community guidelines]

**Move-In Inspection**
We completed the move-in inspection on [Date]. You have a signed copy. Please review it and let me know within 7 days if you notice anything we missed.

**Renter's Insurance**
[If required: Per your lease, please maintain renter's insurance for the duration of your tenancy and provide proof of coverage.]
[If not required: I strongly recommend getting renter's insurance to protect your personal belongings. It's typically $15-30/month.]

Don't hesitate to reach out if you need anything. I want to make sure your experience here is a good one.

Welcome again!

Phil Johnston
[Phone]
[Email]

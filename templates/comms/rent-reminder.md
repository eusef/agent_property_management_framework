# Rent Reminder Templates

**When to use:** Rent is due on the 1st. Send friendly reminder on day 3, formal notice on day 5, late notice on day 10.
**Oregon law note:** Late fees must be "reasonable" and specified in the lease. Oregon does not cap late fees by statute but courts may find excessive fees unenforceable.

---

## Day 3 - Friendly Reminder

Subject: Friendly reminder - [Month] rent

Hi [Tenant Name],

This is a quick reminder that rent for [Month] ($[Amount]) was due on the 1st. If you've already sent payment, please disregard this message.

Accepted payment methods: [Zelle/Check/etc.]

Let me know if you have any questions.

Best,
Phil Johnston

---

## Day 5 - Formal Notice

Subject: [Month] rent - payment not yet received

Hi [Tenant Name],

I haven't yet received your rent payment of $[Amount] for [Month], which was due on [Month] 1st. Please arrange payment as soon as possible.

Per your lease agreement, a late fee of $[Late Fee Amount] applies to payments received after the [Grace Period Day]th of the month.

If there are circumstances affecting your ability to pay, please reach out so we can discuss.

Best,
Phil Johnston

---

## Day 10 - Late Notice / Final Warning

Subject: Urgent - [Month] rent past due

Hi [Tenant Name],

Your rent of $[Amount] for [Month] remains unpaid as of [Today's Date]. A late fee of $[Late Fee Amount] has been applied per your lease terms.

Please remit payment of $[Total with Late Fee] immediately. If I do not receive payment, I may need to begin formal proceedings as required by Oregon law.

If you are experiencing financial hardship, I encourage you to contact 211 (Oregon helpline) for rental assistance resources. I'm also open to discussing a payment plan if you reach out.

Phil Johnston

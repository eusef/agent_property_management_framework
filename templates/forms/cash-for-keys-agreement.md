# Cash-for-Keys Agreement

**When to use:** When you want a tenant to vacate voluntarily instead of pursuing formal eviction. This is often the faster and cheaper option. Eviction can cost $1,500-$5,000+ in legal fees, lost rent, and property damage. A cash-for-keys offer of $200-$600 (or one month's rent) typically resolves the situation faster and with less property damage.
**Important:** Do NOT use this if eviction has already been filed. Consult your attorney if legal proceedings are underway.

### Terms

1. **Vacate Date:** Tenant agrees to vacate the Premises on or before **[Date]** at **[Time]**.

2. **Condition Requirements:** Tenant agrees to leave the Premises in the following condition:
   - All personal belongings removed
   - All trash and debris removed
   - Unit left in broom-clean condition
   - No intentional damage to the property
   - All appliances, fixtures, and systems left intact and in working condition
   - All keys, garage openers, and access devices returned to Landlord

3. **Payment Amount:** In exchange for voluntarily vacating the Premises per the terms above, Landlord agrees to pay Tenant the sum of **$[Amount]**.

4. **Payment Timing:** Payment will be made by [check / money order / cashier's check] at the time of key return and verification that the above conditions have been met. Payment is NOT made in advance of vacating.

5. **Outstanding Balances:** [Choose one:]
   - [ ] Landlord waives any unpaid rent, fees, or charges owed through the vacate date.
   - [ ] Tenant still owes $[Amount] for [description]. This agreement does not waive that obligation.

6. **Mutual Release:** Upon Tenant's compliance with all terms above and Landlord's payment, both parties release each other from any and all claims related to the tenancy at the Premises, except:
   - Claims for property damage beyond normal wear and tear discovered after vacating
   - Security deposit accounting (handled per state law)

7. **Failure to Comply:** If Tenant fails to vacate by the date and time specified, or fails to meet the condition requirements, this agreement is void and Landlord may pursue all legal remedies available, including eviction.

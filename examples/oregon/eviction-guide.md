# Oregon Eviction Guide

**Purpose:** Step-by-step reference for Oregon eviction procedures. Follow this guide AND consult a lawyer for any contested situation.
**Key principle:** Oregon is a "notice first" state. You must provide proper written notice and wait out the required period before filing in court.

---

## Eviction Types and Notice Periods

| Reason | Notice Type | Notice Period | Statute | Cure Allowed? |
|--------|------------|---------------|---------|---------------|
| Non-payment of rent | 10-Day Notice | 10 days | ORS 90.394 | Yes - tenant can pay in full within 10 days to cure |
| Lease violation (first) | 14/30-Day Notice | 14 days to cure, 30 days to vacate if not cured | ORS 90.392 | Yes - 14 days to fix the violation |
| Lease violation (repeat, same type within 6 months) | 10-Day Notice | 10 days | ORS 90.392 | No cure period for repeat violations |
| No-cause (month-to-month) | 90-Day Notice | 90 days | ORS 90.427 | N/A |
| Illegal activity (drug manufacturing, etc.) | 24-Hour Notice | 24 hours | ORS 90.396 | No |
| Immediate danger | 24-Hour Notice | 24 hours | ORS 90.396 | No |

## Step-by-Step: Non-Payment Eviction

### Step 1: Verify the Facts
- [ ] Confirm rent is actually past due (check income records)
- [ ] Confirm amount owed (include any applicable late fees per lease)
- [ ] Confirm no payment was received and misapplied
- [ ] Note the exact number of days past due

### Step 2: Serve 10-Day Notice (ORS 90.394)
- [ ] Use Oregon official form or attorney-drafted notice
- [ ] Notice must state: amount owed, 10-day deadline to pay, consequence of non-payment
- [ ] Include the exact amount owed (rent + any fees outlined in lease)
- [ ] Serve properly (see Service Methods below)
- [ ] Document the date, time, and method of service
- [ ] Keep a copy of the notice

### Step 3: Wait the Full 10 Days
- [ ] Count begins the day after service (not the day of service)
- [ ] If tenant pays in full within 10 days, the notice is void - accept payment
- [ ] If tenant offers partial payment: you may accept it, but consult a lawyer about whether this waives the notice
- [ ] Do NOT accept partial payment without legal advice

### Step 4: If Not Cured - File FED Complaint
- [ ] 10-day period has expired with no payment
- [ ] File Forcible Entry and Detainer (FED) complaint in circuit court
- [ ] Pay filing fee
- [ ] Court hearing typically scheduled within 7-15 days
- [ ] Strongly recommend hiring an attorney at this point

### Step 5: Court Hearing
- [ ] Bring: lease, notice (with proof of service), payment records, any communication records
- [ ] If judgment in your favor: court issues a judgment of restitution
- [ ] Tenant has 4 days to vacate after judgment
- [ ] If tenant does not vacate: sheriff executes the eviction (landlord may NOT self-help)

## Step-by-Step: Lease Violation Eviction

### Step 1: Document the Violation
- [ ] Identify the specific lease clause being violated
- [ ] Gather evidence (photos, written complaints, records)
- [ ] Determine if this is a first violation or a repeat

### Step 2: Serve 14/30-Day Notice (First Violation)
- [ ] Notice must state: the specific violation, what tenant must do to cure, 14-day cure deadline, and that tenancy terminates in 30 days if not cured
- [ ] Serve properly (see Service Methods below)
- [ ] Document service

### Step 3: Monitor Cure Period
- [ ] If tenant cures within 14 days: notice is void, document the cure
- [ ] If same violation recurs within 6 months: can serve 10-day no-cure notice

### Step 4: If Not Cured - File FED
- [ ] Same process as non-payment (Step 4-5 above)

## Service Methods (ORS 90.155)

Oregon accepts the following methods for serving notices:

| Method | Details | Notes |
|--------|---------|-------|
| Personal delivery | Hand it directly to the tenant | Best method - hardest to dispute |
| First-class mail + posting | Mail to tenant AND post on the door | Add 3 days to the notice period for mailing |
| Post on door + mail | Attach to main entrance AND mail first class | Use if tenant is avoiding personal delivery |

**Important:** Keep proof of service. For personal delivery, have a witness or take a timestamped photo. For mailing, use a certificate of mailing (not certified mail, which can be refused).

## What You Cannot Do

- **No self-help eviction.** You cannot change locks, shut off utilities, remove doors/windows, or remove tenant's belongings. This is illegal in Oregon (ORS 90.375) and can result in penalties of up to 2 months' rent.
- **No retaliation.** You cannot evict a tenant for requesting repairs, complaining to a government agency, or joining a tenant organization (ORS 90.385). If you serve a notice within 6 months of a tenant exercising these rights, it's presumed retaliatory.
- **No discrimination.** Eviction criteria must be applied consistently. See fair-housing-additions.md.

## When to Call a Lawyer

- Any contested eviction (tenant disputes the notice or responds to FED)
- If tenant claims retaliation or discrimination
- If tenant is in a protected class and claims the eviction is pretextual
- If tenant has a Section 8 voucher (additional procedural requirements)
- Any time you're unsure about the process
- Domestic violence situations (tenant may have additional rights)

# Oregon Fair Housing Additions

**Purpose:** Oregon-specific fair housing protections that go beyond the federal Fair Housing Act. Use this alongside the framework's `compliance/fair-housing.md` which covers federal requirements.
**Last reviewed:** 2026-03-12

---

## Oregon Additional Protected Classes

In addition to the 7 federal protected classes (race, color, national origin, religion, sex, familial status, disability), Oregon adds:

| Protected Class | Notes |
|----------------|-------|
| Source of income | Including Section 8 vouchers, SSI, TANF, child support -- landlords MUST accept these |
| Marital status | Cannot prefer married/unmarried tenants |
| Sexual orientation | |
| Gender identity | |
| Domestic violence survivor status | |
| Age (18+) | Cannot discriminate against legal adults based on age |

---

## Source of Income -- Key Oregon Rule

Oregon law prohibits landlords from discriminating based on source of income. This is one of the most practically important Oregon additions:

- You MUST accept Section 8 housing choice vouchers
- You MUST accept SSI, TANF, child support, and other government assistance as qualifying income
- You may still apply the same income-to-rent ratio requirements, but must count all lawful income sources
- You cannot have a blanket policy of refusing government-assisted tenants

## Criminal Background Screening (Oregon Rules)

Oregon limits how criminal history can be used in screening:

1. You CANNOT have a blanket "no criminal history" policy
2. You MUST do an individualized assessment considering:
   - Nature and severity of the offense
   - Time elapsed since the offense
   - Evidence of rehabilitation
   - Whether the offense is relevant to tenancy
3. Certain records cannot be considered:
   - Arrests that did not lead to conviction
   - Expunged records
   - Juvenile records
   - Charges that were dismissed
4. You must give the applicant a chance to explain before denying

---

## Oregon Screening Fee Rules (ORS 90.295)

- You may charge a screening fee, but it cannot exceed your actual screening costs
- You must provide a receipt for the screening fee
- You must provide the applicant with a copy of any screening report used

## Oregon Complaint Process

Oregon fair housing complaints are handled by:

- **Oregon Bureau of Labor and Industries (BOLI)** -- state-level complaints
  - Website: https://www.oregon.gov/boli
  - File within 1 year of the alleged violation
- **HUD** -- federal-level complaints
  - File within 1 year of the alleged violation
- **Private lawsuit** -- tenant can also sue directly

Both BOLI and HUD can investigate and impose penalties. Oregon state penalties are in addition to any federal penalties.

## Portland-Specific Additions

Portland adds additional screening and tenant protection rules on top of state law. See `portland-regs.md` for details, including:

- Must publicly post screening criteria 72 hours before accepting applications
- Cannot reject applicants for lacking SSN or proof of lawful presence
- Must provide written screening decision within 2 weeks
- Cannot inquire about immigration/citizenship status

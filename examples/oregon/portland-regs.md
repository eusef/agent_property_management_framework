# Portland / Multnomah County Landlord Regulations

**Applies to:** Properties within Portland city limits (Multnomah County)
**Purpose:** Portland and Multnomah County layer additional requirements on top of Oregon state law (ORS Chapter 90). This doc covers those local additions only. For state-level rules, see landlord-tenant-law.md.
**Last reviewed:** 2026-03-12
**Status:** Research summary. Needs lawyer review before relying on for legal actions.

---

## 1. Rental Registration (Portland City Code 7.02.890)

Portland requires ALL residential rental properties to be registered annually with the City.

- **Fee:** $70/unit/year (2025 rate; adjusted annually by CPI-W)
- **Filing:** Due with annual business tax return (typically April 15 for calendar year filers)
- **Filed with:** City of Portland Revenue Division
- **Form:** Schedule R (filed with Combined Business Tax Return)
- **Contact:** RevBurRentReg@portlandoregon.gov / (503) 823-5157
- **Exemption:** Units regulated at or below 60% AMI are exempt from the fee
- **Penalty for non-registration:** Subject to penalties and enforcement

Source: https://www.portland.gov/phb/rental-services/residential-rental-registration

## 2. Business Tax Obligations

Rental income is "business activity" subject to Portland and Multnomah County business taxes.

| Tax | Rate | Notes |
|-----|------|-------|
| Portland Business License Tax | 2.6% of net income | All rental income in Portland |
| Multnomah County Business Income Tax (MCBIT) | 2.0% of net income | EXEMPT for landlords with fewer than 10 residential units |
| Rental Registration Fee | $70/unit/year | See Section 1 above |

**Key detail:** Landlords with fewer than 10 total residential units are EXEMPT from MCBIT.

**Filing:** Combined Business Tax Return, due April 15 (calendar year filers). File with Portland Revenue Division.

Source: https://www.portland.gov/revenue/business-tax

## 3. Mandatory Renter Relocation Assistance (Portland City Code 30.01.085)

Portland requires landlords to pay relocation assistance when a tenant is displaced due to:

1. No-cause termination (or qualifying landlord reason termination)
2. Landlord declining to renew a lease on substantially the same terms
3. Rent increase of 10% or more within a rolling 12-month period
4. Termination for qualifying landlord reason (demolition, major renovation, owner move-in)

**Payment amounts (by unit size):**

| Unit Size | Relocation Payment |
|-----------|--------------------|
| Studio / SRO | $2,900 |
| 1-bedroom | $3,300 |
| 2-bedroom | $4,200 |
| 3-bedroom+ | $4,500 |

**Timing:**
- No-cause/qualifying landlord reason: pay at least 45 days before termination date
- Rent increase 10%+: tenant has 45 calendar days to request; landlord must pay within 31 days of request

**Reporting:** Notify Portland Housing Bureau (PHB) within 30 days of making any relocation assistance payment.

**Exemptions (12 categories):** Week-to-week tenancies, co-occupied units, owner-occupied duplexes, ADUs where owner lives on-site, and others.

Source: https://www.portland.gov/phb/rental-services/renter-relocation-assistance

## 4. Security Deposit Rules (Portland-Specific)

Portland adds restrictions beyond ORS 90.300:

| Rule | Portland Limit |
|------|----------------|
| Max deposit (no last month's rent required) | 1 month's rent |
| Max deposit (if last month's rent also required) | 0.5 month's rent (on top of last month) |
| Depreciation required for damage charges | YES - must account for age/wear of damaged items |

**2026 change (HB 3521):** Hold deposits can only be collected after approving the application (effective 01/01/2026).

**2026 change (HB 2134):** If a tenant does not proceed with a rental agreement due to habitability defects (ORS 90.320), the landlord must return the security deposit.

Source: https://www.portland.gov/code/30/01/087

## 5. Termination and Eviction (Portland Additions)

Portland layers on top of Oregon's ORS 90.427:

| Rule | State (ORS 90) | Portland Addition |
|------|----------------|-------------------|
| No-cause termination (after year 1) | Prohibited | Same - prohibited |
| Qualifying landlord reason notice | 90 days | 90 days + relocation assistance ($4,500 for 3-bed+) |
| Sale of property notice | 60 days + 1 month rent (effective 09/25/2025 statewide) | Does NOT apply in Portland per City Code 30.01.085/086 - must follow Portland rules instead |
| Tenant rights notice | Not required by state | Required - must include notification of tenant's rights and responsibilities with all termination notices |

**2026 change (HB 2134):** If a tenant gives 30-day notice after receiving a no-cause termination, the landlord cannot collect a lease-break fee or rent beyond the tenant's termination date (effective 01/01/2026).

Source: https://www.portland.gov/phb/rental-services/helpdesk/landlord-tenant-policy-changes

## 6. Rent Increase Rules (Portland)

State law (ORS 90.323) sets the cap, but Portland adds notice and relocation requirements:

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Statewide cap (2026) | 9.5% (for buildings 15+ years old) |
| Notice required | 90 days written notice (state law) |
| Relocation trigger | 10%+ increase in rolling 12-month period triggers mandatory relocation assistance |
| First-year increase | Prohibited (state law) |

Source: https://www.portland.gov/phb/rental-services/helpdesk/landlord-tenant-policy-changes

## 7. Tenant Screening (Portland City Code 30.01.086)

Portland imposes specific requirements for screening applicants:

- Must publicly post all rental criteria, screening criteria, rental amount, and deposit amount BEFORE accepting applications
- If charging a screening fee: must describe the factors you will consider in evaluating applicants
- Must publish screening notices at least 72 hours before accepting applications
- Cannot reject an applicant for lacking a Social Security number or proof of lawful presence
- Must accept rent assistance and public benefits as proof of income (including Section 8 vouchers)
- Must provide written acceptance, conditional acceptance, or denial within 2 weeks of evaluation
- Cannot inquire about or disclose immigration/citizenship status (SB 599, enacted 2025)

Source: https://www.portland.gov/code/30/01/086

## 8. Required Disclosures

Beyond state-required disclosures, Portland landlords must provide:

- Lead paint disclosure (for pre-1978 buildings only)
- Floodplain disclosure (if applicable)
- Smoking policy disclosure
- Accessible Dwelling Unit disclosure (if applicable)
- Name and address of financial institution holding security deposit
- Whether deposit is in interest-bearing account

## 9. Compliance Checklist for Portland Properties

When onboarding a Portland rental property, confirm or complete all of the following:

- [ ] Register the rental unit with Portland (Schedule R + $70/unit fee)
- [ ] Set up Revenue Division tax account for Portland BLT (2.6%)
- [ ] Verify Multnomah County MCBIT exemption (fewer than 10 units)
- [ ] Confirm security deposit is in a trust account (not commingled)
- [ ] Get copy of current lease to verify deposit amount and terms
- [ ] Review current rent vs. 9.5% cap for any future increase planning
- [ ] Prepare tenant screening criteria (for future vacancies)
- [ ] Create a tenant rights notification template (required with any termination notice)
- [ ] Budget relocation assistance reserve (in case ever needed)

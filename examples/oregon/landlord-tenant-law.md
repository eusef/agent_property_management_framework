# Oregon Landlord-Tenant Law Summary

**Purpose:** Quick reference for key Oregon landlord-tenant regulations. This is a summary, not legal advice. Consult a lawyer for complex situations.
**Primary statute:** Oregon Residential Landlord and Tenant Act (ORS Chapter 90)
**Last reviewed:** 2026-03-12

---

## Security Deposits (ORS 90.300)

- No statutory limit on deposit amount (but must be reasonable)
- Must be held in a trust account (not commingled with operating funds)
- Itemized accounting required within 31 days of tenancy end
- Can deduct for damage beyond normal wear and tear, unpaid rent, and cleaning if needed to restore to move-in condition
- If landlord fails to return within 31 days, tenant may recover up to 2x the deposit amount
- Prepaid rent is separate from security deposit

## Rent and Payments

- Rent is due as specified in the lease (typically 1st of month)
- Late fees must be outlined in the lease and be "reasonable"
- No statutory grace period (but many leases include 3-5 days)
- Landlord must provide a receipt if tenant pays in cash
- Oregon does not allow landlords to require electronic-only payment

## Rent Increases (ORS 90.323)

- 90 days written notice required for any rent increase
- Rent control applies to buildings 15+ years old: maximum increase is 7% + annual CPI
- Newly constructed buildings (less than 15 years) are exempt from rent control cap
- No rent increase allowed during the first year of tenancy
- Rent increase notices must be in writing

## Entry and Access (ORS 90.322)

- 24 hours written notice required for non-emergency entry
- Entry only during reasonable hours (typically 8 AM - 6 PM)
- Emergency entry requires no notice (fire, flood, safety hazard)
- Tenant cannot unreasonably deny access for repairs, inspections, or showings

## Habitability Standards (ORS 90.320)

Landlord must maintain:
- Weatherproofing (roof, walls, windows, doors)
- Plumbing in working order (hot and cold water)
- Heating adequate for the climate
- Electrical systems in safe working condition
- Sanitary conditions (trash removal, pest control)
- Working smoke and CO detectors
- Locks on all exterior doors and windows
- Common areas clean and safe

## Repairs and Maintenance

- Tenant must notify landlord of needed repairs in writing (recommended)
- Landlord must respond to essential service failures within a reasonable time
- Essential services: heat, water, electricity, plumbing, security
- Tenant remedies for landlord failure: repair-and-deduct (up to $300 or 1 month rent), rent reduction, or termination
- Landlord may not retaliate against tenant for requesting repairs

## Lease Termination

### By Tenant
- Fixed-term lease: honor the term unless early termination clause exists
- Month-to-month: 30 days written notice
- Domestic violence: tenant can terminate with 14 days notice (ORS 90.453)

### By Landlord
- Month-to-month (no cause): 90 days written notice in Portland metro area
- Month-to-month (with cause): notice period depends on cause (see eviction-guide.md)
- Fixed-term: cannot terminate without cause before lease end (with limited exceptions)
- Qualifying landlord reasons for no-cause: demolition, owner move-in, major renovation (must pay relocation assistance in some jurisdictions)

## Discrimination Protections

Oregon protected classes include all federal classes plus: source of income (including Section 8), marital status, familial status, sexual orientation, gender identity, and domestic violence survivor status. See fair-housing-additions.md.
